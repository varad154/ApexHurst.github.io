Ultimate Trading Plan

1. Define Your Trading Goals

Weekly Goal:

Set a realistic percentage gain per week (e.g., 2-5%). Avoid chasing higher returns to minimize unnecessary risks.

Monthly Goal:

Track your performance monthly to evaluate and adjust as needed.

2. Market Analysis Framework

Technical Analysis:

Utilize tools like Support and Resistance, Trendlines, Moving Averages (MA), and indicators like RSI, MACD, or ATR for volatility tracking.

Fundamental Analysis:

Stay informed on economic data relevant to GBPJPY (UK and Japan) and XAUUSD (primarily U.S. economic indicators).

Sentiment Analysis:

Track factors like risk-on/risk-off sentiment, which greatly impacts GBPJPY and XAUUSD.

3. Timeframe Selection

Primary Timeframe: 4H and Daily for overall trend analysis.

Entry Timeframe: 1H for entry opportunities, aligned with primary trend.

Confirmation Timeframe: 15M to fine-tune entries.

4. Entry and Exit Strategy

Entry Criteria:

  • Look for a pullback to key levels (support/resistance or moving averages).
  • Wait for a reversal candlestick pattern (engulfing, pin bar) or indicator confirmation.
  • Ensure the risk-to-reward ratio is at least 1:2.

Exit Criteria:

  • Use ATR or recent price action levels to set take-profit and stop-loss points.
  • For GBPJPY, a stop-loss of 20-30 pips; take-profit of 50-100 pips.
  • For XAUUSD, a stop-loss of 10-15 pips; take-profit of 20-30 pips.

5. Risk Management

Risk per Trade: Risk no more than 1-2% of capital per trade.

Daily Loss Limit: Set a daily loss limit (e.g., 4%).

Position Sizing: Calculate position size based on pip value and trade risk.

6. Trading Routine

Pre-Market Analysis:

Conduct technical analysis on both GBPJPY and XAUUSD to identify key levels and volatility.

Active Monitoring:

For day trading, monitor price action closely around major sessions.

Post-Market Review:

Review trades daily to adjust strategies as needed.

7. Journaling

Log all trades with entry/exit points, stop-loss, take-profit, and notes on trade rationale.

Review weekly to assess accuracy and improve.

8. Psychological Discipline

  • Set Realistic Expectations: Don’t focus on high returns; instead, prioritize risk management and consistent growth.
  • Avoid Overtrading: Stick to your plan’s entry criteria and avoid forcing trades.
  • Adaptability: Be ready to adapt strategies as market conditions change. For example, increase caution around major economic events impacting GBPJPY and XAUUSD.

Example Plan for GBPJPY and XAUUSD

GBPJPY Example Trade

Entry: Key support level, 4H timeframe, bullish candlestick confirmation.

Stop-Loss: 25 pips below support.

Take-Profit: 70 pips at recent resistance, 1:2 ratio.

XAUUSD Example Trade

Entry: Breakout or pullback near 1H support/resistance.

Stop-Loss: 15 pips below recent low.

Take-Profit: 30 pips at resistance level, 1:2 ratio.
